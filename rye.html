Rye, East Sussex

February 7, 2020

Leaving from King's Cross St. Pancras International station in the morning, it only took 1hr and 9min to arrive in Rye. A small English town near the coast of East Sussex, Rye has been described as a well-preserved medevial town. It's cobbled streets and half-timbered houses are quaint, and the street & house names (Mermaid Street? Gull Cottage & Cannon House?) were even more charming.

Greeted by the sound of seagulls, we wandered aimlessly towards the center. No Google Maps needed as the entire town can be walked in about 20-30 minutes. Notable sites are the Ypres Tower, built in the 14th Century as defense against the French, and Lamb House which was the former residence of author Henry James.
